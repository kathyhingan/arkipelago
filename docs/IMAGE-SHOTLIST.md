## Bohol & Cebu

- [ ] `bohol-cebu-hero.jpg` — **HERO** (wide landscape, ~1600×1000). Signature establishing shot of the destination.
- [ ] `bohol-chocolate-hills.jpg` — **Chocolate Hills** (Carmen, Bohol). 1,268 conical mounds rising from the plains, best seen from the viewing deck before seven in the morning, when the light is soft and the tour groups have not yet arrived.
- [ ] `bohol-tarsier.jpg` — **Philippine Tarsier** (Corella Sanctuary). The world's smallest primate, clinging to a branch two feet from your face. No flash, voices low, and the quieter morning window arranged so the forest is still at its most alert.
- [ ] `bohol-loboc-river.jpg` — **Loboc River** (Private Boat · Bohol). A slow journey by private boat through forest, farmland, and village, with lunch taken on the water. The stillness of a place that has never been asked to perform for visitors.
- [ ] `bohol-balicasag.jpg` — **Balicasag Island** (Bohol Sea · Wall Dive). One of the finest wall dives in the Central Philippines, dropping steeply into deep blue with resident sea turtles and visibility regularly past twenty metres.
- [ ] `bohol-panglao.jpg` — **Panglao Island** (Beaches &amp; Reef). Flat-calm, shallow, white-sand water with easy reef snorkelling straight off the sand. Arkipelago uses the quieter stretches away from the Alona Beach strip.
- [ ] `cebu-basilica-santo-nino.jpg` — **Basilica del Santo Niño** (Cebu City). The oldest Catholic church in the Philippines, home to the 16th-century image of the child Jesus brought by Magellan, drawing pilgrims and travellers with equal reverence.
- [ ] `cebu-magellans-cross.jpg` — **Magellan's Cross** (Cebu City). Planted in 1521 and still standing in its original location, marking the moment the Philippines entered the history of European exploration.
- [ ] `cebu-fort-san-pedro.jpg` — **Fort San Pedro** (Cebu Harbour). The oldest triangular bastion fort in the country, looking out over a harbour that once received galleons from Spain and China alike.

## Batanes

- [x] `batanes-hero.jpg` — hero (filled from existing library; upgrade optional).
- [ ] `batanes-rolling-hills.jpg` — **Rolling Hills of Batan** (Batan Island). Improbably green, rounded hills traversed by roads that wind through hillside farmland. In late afternoon the hills turn a green with no accurate photographic equivalent.
- [ ] `batanes-stone-houses.jpg` — **Ivatan Stone Houses** (Vernacular Architecture). Thick-walled, low-roofed, grass-thatched structures the Ivatan have built for centuries, engineered to survive the most powerful typhoons in the Pacific.
- [ ] `batanes-chavayan-village.jpg` — **Chavayan Village** (Sabtang Island). The most intact Ivatan stone house village, its traditional structures arranged along narrow paths in a landscape that looks untouched since the 18th century.
- [ ] `batanes-valugan-boulder-beach.jpg` — **Valugan Boulder Beach** (East Coast · Batan). A beach made entirely of volcanic boulders worn smooth by centuries of Pacific surf, set against hills and open ocean, with the sound of the surf through the stones unlike any sand beach.
- [ ] `batanes-cape-egmokhan.jpg` — **Cape Egmokhan** (Northernmost Point). The northernmost point of the Philippines, where a short clifftop walk brings you to the edge of the landmass, open water to the north and Taiwan beyond the horizon.
- [ ] `batanes-honesty-coffee-shop.jpg` — **Honesty Coffee Shop** (Ivana · Honour System). A roadside stall in Ivana with no attendant and a tin for payment, not a novelty but simply how things work here, the intact Ivatan honour system made visible.
- [ ] `batanes-faluwa-crossing.jpg` — **Faluwa Crossing** (Batan to Sabtang). The roughly 30-minute boat crossing from Batan to Sabtang, where very few visitors go and the most intact stone house villages survive.

## Bukidnon Highlands

- [x] `bukidnon-hero.jpg` — hero (filled from existing library; upgrade optional).
- [ ] `bukidnon-higaonon-village.jpg` — **Higaonon Village** (Living Tribal Culture). Among the most intact indigenous communities in the Philippines, visited through community liaisons who have built relationships on genuine mutual respect rather than transactional tourism.
- [ ] `bukidnon-talaandig-weaving.jpg` — **Talaandig Weaving** (Indigenous Craft). Weaving, ritual, music, and oral tradition that continue not as performance for visitors but as the actual fabric of daily life among the Talaandig people.
- [ ] `bukidnon-del-monte-plantation.jpg` — **Del Monte Plantation** (Valencia · Since 1926). A pineapple plantation operating here since the 1920s, its regular rows stretching to the mountains and producing a visual effect unlike anything else in the country.
- [ ] `bukidnon-kitanglad-range.jpg` — **Kitanglad Range** (Birdwatching). The plateau's northern wall and one of the most important birdwatching sites in the Philippines, with more than 300 species recorded, including numerous endemics found nowhere else.
- [ ] `bukidnon-philippine-eagle.jpg` — **Philippine Eagle** (National Bird). One of the largest and most endangered raptors on the planet, the national bird holds breeding territory within the Kitanglad range above the highlands.
- [ ] `bukidnon-dahilayan-forest-park.jpg` — **Dahilayan Forest Park** (Manolo Fortich · 1,500m). A pine forest at 1,500 metres where the temperature drops the moment you enter, home to a series of zip-lines, including one of the longest in Asia, running through the canopy.
- [ ] `bukidnon-cagayan-de-oro-river.jpg` — **Cagayan de Oro River** (White-Water Rafting). The headwaters of the country's longest river rise in these highlands, and white-water rafting as it descends toward the coast is one of the more dramatic experiences in Northern Mindanao.

## Vigan & Ilocos

- [x] `vigan-ilocos-hero.jpg` — hero (filled from existing library; upgrade optional).
- [ ] `vigan-cathedral.jpg` — **Vigan Cathedral** (Bell Tower · 1641). The bell tower, built in 1641 and rebuilt to accommodate seismic activity by standing apart from the main structure, is visible from almost every point in the historic centre.
- [ ] `vigan-kalesa.jpg` — **Private Kalesa** (Heritage Quarter). The horse-drawn carriage that is the traditional transport of the Ilocos region, still in daily use. Arkipelago arranges private carriages at the quieter times of day, with your guide providing architectural and historical context.
- [ ] `vigan-syquia-mansion.jpg` — **Syquia Mansion** (Presidential Home). The preserved ancestral home of President Elpidio Quirino, where original furniture and family materials convey what elite Filipino colonial domestic life actually looked like.
- [ ] `vigan-pagburnayan-pottery.jpg` — **Pagburnayan Pottery** (Burnay Kilns). The kilns on the Mestizo River have fired the same dark ceramic vessels in the same tradition for four centuries. A working pottery operation where the techniques used in 1700 are used today.
- [ ] `ilocos-paoay-church.jpg` — **Paoay Church** (Ilocos Norte · UNESCO). A UNESCO World Heritage Site completed in 1710, the finest example of Earthquake Baroque architecture in the Philippines. At sunset, when light falls on the volcanic stone from the west, it is genuinely extraordinary.
- [ ] `ilocos-sand-dunes.jpg` — **Ilocos Sand Dunes** (Paoay). A field of coastal dunes near Paoay Lake reaching roughly three kilometres inland, among the largest sand formations in the Philippines, with late-afternoon light photographers travel considerable distances for.
