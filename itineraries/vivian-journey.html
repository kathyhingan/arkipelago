What you told us

This is the first trip to the Philippines you'll take together, your partner's first visit, and your first time travelling abroad as a couple. You want it to be a journey worth remembering, not a checklist.

You asked for one seamless arrangement covering everything from the moment you land until you fly home: transfers, domestic flights, the finest accommodation, private island days, the experiences worth having, and someone reachable throughout so you never have to organise a thing yourselves. You named Palawan and Boracay, across roughly two weeks, for two.

So we've done exactly that. What follows is one journey, shown at three levels of comfort and privacy. Every element is a starting point, and every element moves once we've spoken.

The Journey

One route, three ways to travel it

The path stays the same at every level: north up the islands, from the wide beaches of Boracay to the quiet lagoons of Palawan, ending among the shipwrecks and limestone of Coron. What changes between tiers is not how much you do. It is how privately you do it, how much of each day is yours to waste, and where you lay your head at night. Tap between the three to see how each one feels.

Refined
Day 1
Arrival, and the first sunset
Manila → Boracay
You land in Manila and connect on a direct flight to Caticlan, where a private vehicle and boat carry you across to Boracay. By late afternoon you're on the sand with nothing left to do. White Beach at sunset is the gentlest possible way to begin, and the walk along it is the only item on the agenda.
Days 2 to 3
Two days to arrive properly
Boracay
Boracay does one thing better than anywhere: it lets you slow down without asking. Two open days for the beach, the spa, and the long lunches that jet lag makes necessary. On either morning, a private island hopping trip is yours if you want it, out to the quieter coves and snorkel reefs away from the main stretch. If you'd rather not move, that is also the right answer.
Island hopping is always private and always optional. Nothing here is scheduled for you unless you want it to be.
Day 4
South to Palawan's gateway
Boracay → Puerto Princesa
A morning flight brings you to Puerto Princesa, the start of the Palawan stretch. The afternoon is easy: settle in, a first taste of Palawan cooking, an early night before tomorrow's centrepiece.
Our Signature Experience
Day 5
In search of the whale sharks
Puerto Princesa
This is the day we build the Palawan gateway around. A private boat carries you out into the plankton-rich waters off Puerto Princesa in search of the gentle giants of the Sulu Sea, the whale sharks, the largest fish in the ocean and entirely harmless. Your crew know where they feed and how to read the season, and when one glides up out of the blue you slip into the water beside it, on your own terms, at a respectful distance. It is the encounter people fly across the world for, and here it is yours privately. In the afternoon, the UNESCO-listed Puerto Princesa Subterranean River, a navigable river running straight through a mountain, is there for those who want a second wonder in one day.
Our signature experience. The whale shark expedition is fully private and timed to the calmest morning water, when sightings are at their best. Sightings are wild and never guaranteed, but Puerto Princesa is among the finest places in the country to seek them.
Day 6
North, to where it goes quiet
Puerto Princesa → Port Barton
A private drive north, about three hours through rice country and forest, delivers you to Port Barton: a small bay with no crowds and no rush, the Palawan that El Nido was twenty years ago. The afternoon asks nothing of you.
Day 7
Sandbars and sea turtles
Port Barton
Port Barton's water is the reason to be here. A private boat, if you want one, out to the reefs where sea turtles feed, the vanishing sandbars, and the empty beaches you'll likely have to yourselves. Or the hammock. The hammock is a legitimate plan.
Day 8
On to the famous bay
Port Barton → El Nido
A shorter private drive, about two hours, up to El Nido. You base yourselves on the calmer edge of town, away from the noise, within reach of Bacuit Bay and its limestone.
Days 9 to 11
The lagoons, at the right hour
El Nido
Three days in the bay that put Palawan on every map. Your island hopping through Bacuit Bay is private, which is the whole difference: you reach the Big and Small Lagoons in the early light or the late afternoon, when the day boats have gone and the water is yours. Between water days there's Nacpan Beach, a long curve of sand up the coast, and the good kind of doing nothing.
One of these three days is deliberately left open. No boat, no plan. It's the day the trip becomes a rest, not a tour.
Day 12
The short flight to the shipwrecks
El Nido → Coron
A brief flight across to Coron, the most dramatic water in the Philippines. The afternoon is gentle: the Maquinit hot springs, warm saltwater at dusk, a slow first evening.
Days 13 to 14
Lakes inside islands
Coron
Coron closes the journey with its best hand: a private boat day to Kayangan Lake, the clearest water you'll ever stand in, ringed by karst; Twin Lagoon, entered under a limestone wall; and the shallow Japanese shipwrecks, close enough to the surface to snorkel over. A second Coron day is yours to shape, whether that's another island run to Malcapuya's white sand or a final slow morning.
Day 15
Home, changed a little
Coron → Manila
A private transfer to the airport and your flight to Manila, connecting to your journey home. You leave having seen five distinct corners of one country, and none of them the crowded version.
