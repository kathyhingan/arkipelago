Three Journeys for Susan Fromer

2 Adults · 15–23 Days · January 20, 2027 · Source: Arkipelago Homepage Quote Form

The brief: Beach, mountains, city, and countryside, a total experience. Active but not rushed: hiking yes, diving no, mild snorkelling only. Wellness retreat included. Food tours, culture, and local interaction are the priority throughout, this couple are foodies and every leg should reflect that. Luxury but not extravagant. Beach days are for rest, not itineraries, private island hopping only if they want it, never scheduled. Susan's partner has motion sensitivity, every transfer below is routed to minimise rough water and turbulence. They've said plainly they don't enjoy the mechanics of travel, so each journey favours fewer, longer stays over constant movement. Investment estimates below are all-in ranges for two, pending final property and flight confirmation.

Three Ways Into the Same Story

How to read this document

Each journey below is a complete route, a different way of stitching together wellness, coastline, and heritage across 2.5 to 3 weeks. Within each journey, three tiers show how the same route deepens as accommodation and pace are elevated: more time in each place, fewer moving parts, more that is arranged exclusively for the two of them. Nothing here is fixed. Pick a route, a tier, or pieces of both, we'll build the final version around exactly what Susan and her partner want after a call.

Journey One

El Nido & The North

This route trades constant movement for depth: a wellness reset inland, one long stay on the water at Seda Lio in El Nido, and a slower final week in Vigan's Spanish colonial north. It's the most restful of the three routes, fewer transfers, longer stays, and a rhythm built entirely around rest, food, and walking rather than logistics.

Tier 1
Day 1
Arrival: A Taste of Manila
Private airport meet-and-greet, no queues, straight to the hotel. That afternoon, a private guide walks you through Intramuros, the old walled city, cobblestone streets, Spanish-era churches, and the bastion walls, timed for late day once the crowds have thinned. From there it's a short crossing into Binondo, the world's oldest Chinatown, for a walking food trail through its side streets: century-old dumpling houses, fresh lumpia, and the noodle shops that have fed the district for generations.
[FOODIE NOTE: Binondo rewards moving between four or five small, unglamorous places rather than one sit-down restaurant. This is the one evening built around grazing, not a table.]
Day 2
South to the Farm
A two-hour private drive south into Batangas, the city dropping away into coconut groves and volcano views. Arrival at [ACCOMMODATION: The Farm at San Benito, Lipa, wellness suite tier] in time for a late lunch from their organic garden kitchen.
[TRAVEL NOTE: This leg is entirely overland, no boats, no flights, the smoothest transfer in the entire journey.]
Days 3–4
The Wellness Reset
Two unhurried days built around the Farm's own rhythm. Day 3 stays entirely on the property: a guided forest trail on the lower slopes of Mt. Malarayat in the cool of the morning, hydrotherapy and native massage in the afternoon, plant-based tasting menus built from what was picked that morning. Day 4 crosses into Tagaytay, forty minutes north, for the overlook across Taal Lake and its volcano island rising out of the water, with dinner that evening at Balay Dako, the Filipino comfort-food house known as much for its cooking as for that same view across the caldera.
[FOODIE NOTE: Balay Dako's menu is built around Filipino home cooking done properly, kare-kare, crispy pata, grilled seafood, the kind of table this couple will actually want after two lighter days at the Farm.]
Day 5
North to the Lagoon Coast
Private transfer to Manila's domestic terminal, then a direct flight to El Nido, the calmest scheduled departure of the day, chosen specifically for smoother air. A private transfer delivers you to [ACCOMMODATION: Seda Lio, El Nido, beachfront suite] by early afternoon, with the rest of the day entirely open.
[TRAVEL NOTE: We book the earliest, calmest-weather flight window into El Nido and request a seat away from the propellers, the smoothest configuration available on this route.]
Days 6–10
Lio Beach: Five Days of Doing Very Little
This stretch has no fixed programme. Lio Beach itself is calm and shallow, good for slow mornings and no currents to think about. Bacuit Bay has more calm, sheltered islands than most guests ever see, and on the two or three mornings you choose, a private outrigger can reach any combination of them: the Big and Small Lagoons and the Secret Lagoon for flat, glass-still water with mild snorkelling over the reef edges, Shimizu Island for one of the bay's best coral gardens in shallow, protected water, Star Beach and Helicopter Island for wide sweeps of sand with almost no one on them outside peak hours, and the Matinloc Shrine and Hidden Beach for a quieter, more scenic run further south. None of it is more strenuous than a short swim from the boat. The rest of the days are yours: the beach, the Lio Estate's small design district for coffee and browsing, long lunches. Dinners rotate between the resort's own kitchen and two or three private chef set-ups on the sand, arranged the day of.
[FOODIE NOTE: El Nido's better tables are small and reservation-only, we'll pre-arrange two dinners rather than leave them to chance.]
[TRAVEL NOTE: Island hopping here uses a single stable outrigger, not a shared speedboat tour, and only on the days you choose, this is never scheduled by default.]
Day 11
North to Vigan
Morning flight back to Manila, connecting same-day to a short flight north to Laoag, then a 40-minute private transfer into Vigan. This is the routing we'd recommend by default: the overland alternative runs closer to seven hours by private vehicle and adds a full day of driving neither of you needs. Afternoon arrival at [ACCOMMODATION: heritage boutique hotel, Vigan UNESCO quarter].
[TRAVEL NOTE: Flying via Laoag keeps this connection under two hours in the air combined, against a full day overland. Given the preference to avoid unnecessary travel time, this is the routing we'd book by default.]
Days 12–13
Cobblestones and Countryside
Morning walks through Calle Crisologo before the day-trippers arrive, a kalesa ride through the old quarter, and a full afternoon food tour, Vigan longganisa, bagnet, empanada at the stall your guide has used for fifteen years. One day extends into the Ilocos countryside: the Kapurpurawan rock formations, Paoay's colonial church, and a short, flat hike up to the Bantay Bell Tower viewpoint for sunset over the plain.
[FOODIE NOTE: Vigan is arguably the strongest food destination on this entire route for a couple who cook and eat seriously, we'd suggest a private cooking session with a local family as an easy addition.]
Departure transfer to Manila for the international flight home closes Day 13.

Journey Two

Coron & The Visayas

Coron trades El Nido's open-water drama for something calmer and, for this couple specifically, more comfortable: its signature experiences are inland lakes and sheltered bays rather than long open-sea crossings, and the town itself has a quieter, less trafficked feel. This route pairs it with Bohol and Cebu, hills, hiking, and one of the country's strongest food cities, for the "mountains and city" half of the brief that Journey One leaves lighter.

Days 6–9
Coron: Lakes Before the Sea
Coron's signature experience is Kayangan Lake, flat, sheltered, freshwater, no swell at all, reached by a short private outrigger ride across calm harbour water rather than open sea. The neighbouring Twin Lagoon, split by a limestone wall you can swim or paddle through at low tide, is calm enough to pair with it on the same morning. One morning, a moderate hike up Mt. Tapyas for sunset views over the town and harbour, well within an easygoing pace. The rest of the days are open, with a wider run of calm islands to choose from as the mood strikes: the wide, shallow reef at Siete Pecados for the mildest snorkelling in the area, the white sand at CYC and Banol Beach, and Malcapuya and Bulog Dos further out for a full day of quiet, protected water and almost no other boats. Nothing here is scheduled by default.
[FOODIE NOTE: Coron town has a genuinely good, low-key seafood scene, we'll arrange one dinner where your guide simply walks you to the market to choose the catch, then to the grill stall of their choice.]
Day 10
South to Bohol
Private transfer to Busuanga airport, connecting via Manila or Cebu to Tagbilaran. Private transfer to [ACCOMMODATION: 4-star resort, Panglao, Bohol].
Days 11–13
Bohol's Hills and Cebu's Table
An early departure to beat the crowds at the Chocolate Hills viewpoint, followed by a gentle countryside walk through the surrounding farmland and a slow lunch cruise on the Loboc River, calm, flat water throughout. A day trip crosses to Cebu City for its food scene specifically: lechon at the source, a street food crawl through Carbon Market with a guide who knows which stalls matter, and a heritage walk through the old Spanish quarter.
[FOODIE NOTE: Cebu is the strongest single food day of this entire journey, we'd suggest keeping the Bohol-to-Cebu day light on sightseeing and heavy on eating.]
Days 14–15
Final Beach Days
Back at the resort in Panglao with nothing scheduled, the beach, the pool, a final private dinner on the sand. Departure transfer to Manila for the international flight home closes Day 16.
