The Journey

Sixteen Nights, Three Seas

Manila
December 18 · One night
Arrival
December 18
A Soft Landing
You arrive into Manila in the evening and are met the moment you clear the airport, then driven to your hotel for the night. Nothing is asked of you today. Sleep off the flight. The islands begin in the morning.
Cauayan Island Resort, El Nido
December 19 to 22 · Three nights
Bacuit Archipelago
December 19 to 22
The First Water
A morning flight carries you north to El Nido, where a boat brings you across to Cauayan Island and the mainland falls away behind you. Your days here move at whatever pace you and your partner set. When you want the water, a private bangka takes you into the outer Bacuit Archipelago early, before the day boats leave the bay, so the lagoons are yours: the quiet coves, a low-tide sandbar, reef bright enough to keep you in the water until lunch, which is laid out on an island with no one else on it.
A note on pace. Every island day here is private and entirely optional. Some mornings the finest plan is the view from your own terrace and nowhere at all to be.
The Private Expedition, El Nido to Coron
December 22 to 25 · Three nights · Your own boat and crew
Minimal Crowds
December 22 to 25
The Crossing, Privately
On the 22nd you leave El Nido for the stretch of sea most travellers only pass straight through: the Linapacan and Culion islands, strung between El Nido and Coron, where the water turns a colour photographs never quite hold and the beaches carry a single set of footprints, yours. Three days on your own boat with your own crew, no fixed crowd anywhere near you. You wake to a different island each morning, snorkel reefs that see almost no one, and watch the sun drop from the deck with the engine off.
This is the heart of what you asked for. Private, and far from the crowds. How you spend the nights out here, aboard or ashore, is the one choice we will settle with you, so the vessel matches exactly how you want to fall asleep under that sky.
Two Seasons Coron Island Resort & Spa
December 25 to 28 · Three nights
Wrecks & Lagoons
December 25 to 28
Coron, From the Water
The expedition delivers you into Coron and out to your island at Two Seasons. Coron is the drama of this coast: limestone walls dropping straight into the sea, Kayangan Lake at first light before the boats arrive, the strange warm depths of Barracuda Lake, the marine garden at Siete Pecados. For divers, this is where the journey turns serious. The Japanese wrecks off Coron are among the finest anywhere to dive, and Barracuda Lake is a descent unlike any other. One evening, a private dinner is set for the two of you on a quiet beach, cooked to order, lit low, the day boats long gone.
Diving and fishing, set to you. We match the wreck dives to your certification and recent time underwater, so the days below are pitched precisely to the two of you. And if a morning on the open water appeals, giant trevally run off Busuanga, an easy day to arrange with the right boat.
Discovery Coron
December 28 to 30 · Two nights
The Quiet Islands
December 28 to 30
The Soft End of Palawan
You move to Discovery Coron for the last of Palawan, and the rhythm eases. These are the days for the southern islands, Malcapuya, Bulog Dos, Banol, white sand over shallow, clear water, and for a sandbar picnic laid out at low tide with nothing on the horizon. Or for nothing at all, which is its own kind of luxury on a honeymoon.
Across to Bohol
December 30 · Travel day, handled end to end
By Air
December 30
The Move East
On the 30th you cross to Bohol. There is no direct hop between Coron and Panglao, so the smooth way, and the one that keeps you out of a long sea crossing, is to fly the whole way by air through Manila and land at Panglao that same afternoon. A short drive brings you to Amorita, on the cliff above Alona Beach, in time to settle before dinner.
You will not feel the seams. The boat, the drive to the airport, both flights, and the transfer at the far end are arranged as one continuous handover, so the travel disappears and you simply arrive.
Amorita Resort, Panglao
December 30 to January 2 · Three nights
Bohol & New Year
December 30 to January 2
Bohol, and the Year's Turn
Amorita sits above Alona Beach, and your final days are built gently around the water again. A private boat carries you out to Balicasag, where the reef falls away into a wall and turtles move through the blue, and to the Virgin Island sandbar at low tide. And on the 31st, the year turns: a private dinner set apart from everything, to close the trip the way it deserves to be closed.
New Year's Eve is the natural high point of these last days. We build it around exactly the evening you and your partner want, quiet and just the two of you, or something with a little more spark.
Manila, and Home
January 2 to 3 · One night
Departure
January 2 to 3
The Long Way Home
A short flight returns you to Manila for a final night, and the next day you are taken to the airport for the journey home, carrying a honeymoon that touched three of the loveliest corners of the country and crossed the sea between them the way almost no one gets to.

Before We Finalise

The Last Few Brushstrokes

Three small things are still yours to decide, and each one changes how we book the day around it. A short call is the easiest way to settle them, and then this becomes real.

  • The nights on the expedition. Sleeping aboard the boat and waking already out among the islands, or coming ashore each evening. Tell us which you would prefer and we match the right vessel to it.
  • Your diving. Your certification level and how recently you have dived. This is what lets us set the Coron wrecks to your experience, from a gentle first descent to the deeper wrecks that reward it.
  • The fishing. We have read this as giant trevally off the open water near Busuanga. If that is right, we will brief the right boat. If you had something else in mind, tell us.
